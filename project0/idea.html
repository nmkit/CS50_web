Market Problem

Hong Kong has one of the most active property market in the world. However, an active market does not naturally represent a transparent and information symmetric market for buyer and seller. Coupled with the sad situation that HK has been ranked the worldâ€™s least-affordable housing market for the 10th year by Demographia, purchasing a flat usually not only use up all the saving of a young couple but also put them in large debt burden which trap them from chasing their dream. Even worse is that property puchase is inherently an information assymetric eercise with the seller always having a much better understnding of the property(in term of physical properties and encumbrances) than the buyer In a seller market of the past years and the inherent nature of agent in fighting for a higher price's comission, agents may not disclose unfavorable condition of the property.

I felt furious to the market reality that the system is inclined to one side. I want to help make a change by providing more assistance to he buyer for them in making a better informed decision.

My Plan

The first thing I can think of is providng free residential property valuation system deploying my domain knowledge in valuation and the transaction data of the past 20 years.
In view of that, I self-learnt python, extended my secondary statistics knowledge with machine learning algorithm and learning web development to put it for public use. (This site is my first homework for that.) I have finished developing the system in python code and the site for the system is under construction. The mean of absolute error from backtest is around 9%(real error shall be lower as not all non-market transactions is distingusible to be excluded from backtest.) A simulation of purchasing with 5% discount to the system's estimate along the past 10 years from 31 Dec 2019 beat the market(represented by the Centa-City Index) by ~6% on average.
By providing this free valuatino system, I hope that people can have one more reference when making property purchase decision.
